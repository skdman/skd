Traditional Food (Balanced & Human Style)

Nasi Lemak

Often called the heart of Malaysian food, Nasi Lemak is coconut rice served with spicy sambal, crispy anchovies, peanuts, cucumber, and egg. It’s simple, filling, and loved by everyone — whether for breakfast, lunch, or even late-night supper.

Nasi Kerabu

This colorful dish from Kelantan is known for its blue rice, naturally dyed with butterfly pea flowers. Served with fresh herbs, salted egg, crackers, and grilled meat or fish, Nasi Kerabu is a beautiful mix of flavors that represents the unique culture of the East Coast.

Satay

Satay is smoky, juicy, and always a crowd favorite. Skewers of marinated meat are grilled over charcoal and dipped into a rich peanut sauce, usually enjoyed with cucumber, onions, and rice cakes. Found in night markets and celebrations, satay is a true symbol of togetherness.
